About

I am a biomedical data scientist and ML engineer with a track record of shipping models that work in the real world — not just on benchmarks. I'm currently an MS student in Bioengineering at Penn, where I build clinical AI on EHR databases and contribute to computational pathology research. I am supported by the Kwanjeong Foundation Scholarship.

My industry background is at LG Energy Solution, where I spent two years designing and deploying data pipelines and predictive models at scale — including a battery stress scoring system and a preprocessing pipeline adopted across the organization. I know what it takes to move from a Jupyter notebook to a production environment.

My research background spans EHR analysis, time-series modeling, NLP, and medical imaging. I've worked on ICU readmission prediction, women's health epidemiology using nationwide cohort data, and a vision-language model for digital pathology. I'm drawn to problems where messy, real-world biomedical data can be shaped into something clinically meaningful.

I hold a BS in Statistics from Korea University and have filed two patents in battery health monitoring. I'm fluent in Korean and English, and conversational in German.
